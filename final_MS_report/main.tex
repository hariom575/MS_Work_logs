\chapter*{Abstract}
Modern smartphones have become indispensable, yet their constant connectivity and persuasive design often undermine self-control, leading to habitual checking, distraction, and reduced focus. Existing digital-wellbeing tools mainly rely on restrictive controls such as app limits or passive screen-time dashboards. These approaches frequently fail to sustain engagement or encourage meaningful behavioral change.

\textbf{JoDo (Joy of Digital cOntrol)} addresses this gap through a reward-driven, autonomy-supportive approach to mindful smartphone use. The system combines a minimalist launcher, intentional app-access flow, daily reward tiers, and a peer leaderboard. By transforming routine interactions into opportunities for positive feedback, JoDo encourages users to self-regulate without coercion.

The BPS-2 phase focused Mostly on pilot study and improvement on existing features based on pilot study results:
\begin{itemize}
  \item planned and conducted 2 pilot study, each one for two weeks.
  \item Conducted pre-pilot study plus post pilot study interviews.
  \item Made furthere changes in the App's UI based on pilot study feedback.
  \item Fixed bug in the system that appeared during pilot study.
  \item Made the system more secured by integrating https, port blocking, enabling firewall protection and installed NGINX server as a reverse proxy.
  \item Tested the app across various android devices and fixed bugs encountered in those devices.
  
\end{itemize}

Preliminary observations from the pilot study indicate improved awareness of screen-time patterns and strong user motivation to maintain higher reward tiers.  
The planned BPS-3 phase will include a formal user study—conducted to quantitatively assess behavioral outcomes and long-term participation.

\chapter{Introduction}
\section{Background and Motivation}
The number of internet users reached 5.56 billions in 2024, representing 67.9 \% of the global population, with the average daily internet usage growing to 6 hours 38 minutes \citep{datareportal2024digital}. Digital Devices now enable continuous interaction anytime and anywhere; however constant connectivity often generate discomfort or a sense of loss when disconnected - a phenomenon described as \textit{technological unconsciousness}. 

Hoehe and Thibaut (2020) \citep{hoehe2020going} emphasized that individuals must be intentional, creative and mindful in their technology use to enhance relationships and productivity;
otherwise,technology risks eroding rather than supporting quality of life. Technology itself is value neutral - it can be beneficial or harmful depending on how it is applied. When technology intended to improve life is used without restraint, it can ultimately reduce quality of life.

Major technology firms have begun acknowledging this: Google's Digital Well-being suite and Apple's Screen Time both frame digital balance as a design priority, emphasizing user awareness and self-regulation, However, these tools typically target hedonic outcomes - maximizing pleasure and minimizing discomfort - rather than eudemonic outcomes involving meaning and self-realization \citep{hutmacher2022personalization} . Moreover, empirical studies reveal inconsistent effects of such built-in tools on well-being.

\section{Problem Statement}
Existing digital-well-being tools suffer from three key limitations:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item They are passive monitors that do not introduce meaningful friction into habitual phone use.
    \item They lack social and competitive motivation to sustain behavior change.
    \item Their reward models do not connect digital restraint to tangible real-world value.
\end{enumerate}
\section{Project Objectives}
The objectives of the BPS2 project are:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Design, develop, and iteratively improve JoDo, a custom Android launcher that embeds digital well-being interventions intoo core phone usage loop.
    \item Deploy JoDo on the Google Play Store and recruit participants for a pilot study.
    \item Evaluate effectiveness through quantitative and qualitative pilot study methods.
    \item Harden the backend infrastructure with security and rate-limiting measures.
    \item Plan and prepare for a full 2 weeks user study.
\end{enumerate}
